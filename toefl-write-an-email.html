TOEFL Write an Email (2026): Complete Guide

Updated for the January 2026 TOEFL iBT format · 5 min read

The Write an Email task is the second writing task in the 2026 TOEFL iBT. You are given a real-world scenario and must write a professional email of 130-140 words that covers three specific required points.

What Does the Task Look Like?

You receive a scenario, a recipient, and three required points to address. All three must be included or your score drops significantly regardless of writing quality.

Example prompt:
You missed an important lecture last week. Write an email to your professor.
Include: (1) explain why you missed the class, (2) ask for the lecture notes, (3) confirm you will attend the next class.

The Perfect Email Structure

  1. Formal greeting: "Dear Professor Smith," or "Dear Sir/Madam,"
  2. Opening line: State your purpose in one sentence — "I am writing to..."
  3. Body: Address all three required points clearly, one at a time
  4. Polite closing: "I look forward to hearing from you." or "Thank you for your time."
  5. Sign-off: "Sincerely," or "Kind regards," followed by your name

Key Phrases That Score Well

Openings: "I am writing to..." / "I would like to inform you that..."
Requests: "Would it be possible to..." / "I was wondering if you could..."
Apologies: "I sincerely apologize for..." / "I regret to inform you that..."
Closings: "I look forward to your response." / "Thank you for your understanding."

Sample High-Scoring Email

Dear Professor Johnson,

I am writing to apologize for missing your lecture on Tuesday. Unfortunately, I was unwell and had to visit the doctor, which prevented me from attending class.

I would be very grateful if you could share the lecture notes or slides from that session, as I do not want to fall behind on the material. I have already asked a classmate for a brief summary.

I would like to confirm that I will be present for all remaining lectures this semester. Thank you for your understanding.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

5 Tips for a High Score

  • Cover all three required points — missing even one significantly hurts your score
  • Aim for 130-140 words — too short or too long both reduce your score
  • Use formal language — avoid contractions like "I'm" or "can't"
  • Address the recipient by name if given — shows attention to detail
  • End with a clear formal closing — signals task completion to the AI scorer
