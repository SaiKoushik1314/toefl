Understanding the 4 Speaking Tasks

Task 1 — Independent Speaking (45 seconds)

You are given a question about a personal preference, experience, or opinion. You have 15 seconds to prepare and 45 seconds to respond. No reading or listening material is provided.

Example prompts:
• "Describe a place you enjoy spending time in and explain why."
• "Some people prefer to work alone. Others prefer to work in groups. Which do you prefer and why?"
• "Talk about a skill you would like to learn. Explain why you want to learn it."

Template: State your preference → Give 2 reasons → Add a specific example for each → Brief conclusion

Task 2 — Integrated: Campus Announcement (60 seconds)

Read a short campus announcement (45 seconds), then listen to a student's reaction (conversation). Summarise both and explain the student's opinion. 30 seconds to prepare, 60 seconds to speak.

Template:
"The announcement states that [main point]. The [man/woman] [agrees/disagrees] with this because [reason 1] and [reason 2]. For example, [specific detail from audio]."

Task 3 — Integrated: Academic Reading + Lecture (60 seconds)

Read a short academic passage defining a concept (45 seconds), then listen to a professor give an example or contrast. Explain the concept and how the lecture illustrates it. 30 seconds to prepare, 60 seconds to respond.

Template:
"The passage defines [concept] as [definition]. The professor illustrates this with an example of [example]. Specifically, [key detail 1] and [key detail 2]. This demonstrates that [concept] because [connection]."

Task 4 — Integrated: Academic Lecture Only (60 seconds)

Listen to a lecture about an academic concept. Summarise the main points using examples from the lecture. No reading passage. 20 seconds to prepare, 60 seconds to respond.

Template:
"The professor discusses [main topic]. [He/She] explains [first point] and gives the example of [detail]. Additionally, [second point] is illustrated by [example]. Together, these show that [main idea]."

How Speaking is Scored

Each Speaking task is scored on a 1–6 scale across three criteria:

🗣️ Delivery

Fluency, pace, pronunciation, and clarity. Avoid long pauses and speak at a natural, steady pace.

📖 Language Use

Grammar accuracy, vocabulary range, and sentence variety. Use complex structures and avoid repetition.

💡 Topic Development

Completeness, coherence, and use of specific examples. Address all parts of the prompt fully.

Frequently Asked Questions

Task 1: 45 seconds. Tasks 2, 3, and 4: 60 seconds each. Preparation time is 15–30 seconds depending on the task.
Most universities require a 4.0 or above on the new 1–6 scale. Some programs requiring Teaching Assistants may require 5.0 or above on Speaking specifically.
Any clear accent is acceptable. The raters (human and AI) are trained to understand a wide variety of accents. Clarity and fluency matter more than accent.
Occasional filler words won't significantly affect your score, but frequent pauses and fillers hurt your Delivery score. Practice speaking continuously without stopping.
