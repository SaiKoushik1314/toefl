Tips for users

Keep the strategy simple: read for purpose, locate support, eliminate weak choices, and watch for vocabulary in context.

Before you answer

Read for structure first

Identify what the passage is mainly doing: describing a process, explaining a cause, comparing two ideas, or giving instructions.

  • Circle signal words like however, therefore, for example, and in contrast.
  • Notice whether each paragraph has one job.
During questions

Find evidence before choosing

Strong answers are supported by the text. Weak answers sound reasonable but are too broad, too extreme, or not actually stated.

  • Return to the relevant sentence or paragraph.
  • Eliminate choices that add new information.
For vocabulary

Use context, not memory alone

A familiar word may mean something different in a passage. Look at nearby words, tone, and the sentence purpose before choosing.

  • Replace the original word with each option mentally.
  • Check grammar and meaning together.
